BMW M5

The F10 BMW M5 series swapped its predecessor's naturally-aspirated V10 for a twin-turbocharged V8, offering gearheads a generous bump in horsepower and torque. Rear-wheel drive was the only configuration available, but buyers in the United States could choose between a dual-clutch automatic transmission or a 6-speed manual. This makes the new F10 BMW M5 the most useful M5 ever, offering more luxury than ever before while outperforming the more expensive supercars of the era.
